The Plastic Jam


The Plastic Jam - A Pan-India movement to raise awareness about plastic waste management practices and to advocate for efficient and accountable implementation of the Extended Producers Responsibility.

Problem Statement

As per CPCB reports 2011 India produces 15,342 tonnes of plastic waste everyday out of which more than 40% is left unmanaged resulting in over 2.2 million tons of unmanaged plastic waste annually. India with 0.60 million tonnes ranks 12th in the list of 20 countries which contributes 83% of the plastic waste dumped in the oceans. Extended Producers Responsibility had been introduced in the Plastic Waste Management Rules, 2011 and had been largely redefined in PWM 2016 asking the Producers, Importers and Brand Owners who introduce products in the market to take primary responsibility for collection of used multi-layered plastic sachet or pouches or packaging. However, even after 7 years from the introduction of EPR the implementation hasn’t been as effective and regulated.

Mission

Our team is on a mission to mobilise citizens especially Youth from all parts of the country to pledge their support for ending ‘The Plastic Jam’ and consciously take an effort to Reduce the use of plastic, Reuse it and contribute towards management of plastic waste through recycling and segregation of waste and sign a petition asking the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change to take steps on efficient implementation of the Extended Producers Responsibility.

How You Can Contribute:

  • Sign the Petition and Share it on your Social Media
  • Organize/Join the The Plastic Jam Mobilisation in your School or Community
  • Observe the No Plastic Week and Share your story with us
